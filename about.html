FENI DIABETIC ASSOCIATION

Who we are:

Feni Diabetic Association (FDA) is a non-profit voluntary service-oriented organization that aims to support individuals living with diabetes and provide treatment in Bangladesh. It has emerged as a beacon of hope and support for individuals with diabetes and various health disorders. FDA embarked on its journey from 1st October 1993 through the collective efforts of compassionate local social workers and organizers. Since then, it has been actively involved in various initiatives to combat diabetes and positively impact the community.

Late National Professor Dr. Mohammed Ibrahim was a pioneer in revolutionizing diabetic care in our country. Recognizing the complex nature of diabetes, he understood that effective management required active involvement not just from doctors, but also from the patients themselves. Consequently, he envisioned a socio-medical model of care that emphasized the crucial role patients play in managing their condition effectively.

Brief history of FDA:

On August 31, 1993, under the initiative of Abdus Sattar, a prominent businessman, and social worker in Feni, and with the participation of individuals from diverse backgrounds, a preparatory meeting was convened at Shaheed Zahir Raihan Auditorium at Mizan Road. The meeting was chaired by the former Deputy Commissioner of Feni, Iftekhar Ahmed, aimed to establish a branch of the Diabetic Association of Bangladesh in Feni. During the meeting, it was decided to form the Diabetic Association of Bangladesh, Feni branch, and a draft constitution was approved. Additionally an advisory council comprising 24 members, with the Deputy Commissioner of Feni, Iftekhar Ahmed as the president, and an Executive Council comprising 24 members with the Civil Surgeon of Feni, Dr. Shamsul Alam as the president were appointed. Subsequently, on September 1, 1993, at 5:00 pm, the first joint meeting of the newly formed Advisory and Executive Council of the Diabetic Association of Bangladesh, Feni branch, took place in the conference room of the Deputy Commissioner’s office, under his chair. The second joint meeting was held in the Municipality on September 14, 1993 and the third joint meeting arranged on September 29, 1993 also in the Municipality. During these meetings, it was unanimously decided to commence the association's activities formally by renting three rooms from the Municipality, starting from October 1, 1993, and appointing the necessary personnel. Consequently, from 1st October 1993 as a befitting manner Diabetic Association of Bangladesh, Feni branch, inaugurated the medical services program of the branch. The event was attended by the former Deputy Commissioner, Iftekhar Ahmed, Additional District Magistrate Nurul Haque, Additional Deputy Commissioner and Municipal Administrator Debashish Nag, TNO M Samad, Thana Health and family planning Officer Dr. Kazi Ismail Amanullah, along with the General Secretary Abdus Sattar, Vice-President Alhaj Ohidur Rahman, Joint Secretary Dr. A.S.M Tobarok Ullah Chowdhury Bayzied and Harun or Rashid Patwari, Treasurer Alhaj Abdul Kuddus, and other founding members, such as Kobbad Ahmed, Mohammad Elias, Dr. Md. Asad Ullah Farooq, Dr. Daudul Islam, Prof. Abdul Motaleb Bhuiyan, Mostansur Rahman Chowdhury, Abdur Rahman B.Com, Kamal Hasan Chowdhury, Kazi Anwara Hossain, Nasir Uddin Milan, Advocate Abul Kashem (Senior), Advocate Abul Kashem (Junior), Advocate Moulobi Khair Ahmed, Selina Chowdhury, Md. Rehan Uddin, Md. Manjurul Islam, Ali Ahmed, M.A Chowdhury, Abul Kashem Chowdhury, Golam Sarwar Bahar, Golam Nabi, journalist A. Odud, Golam Mostafa Bhuiyan, Nurul Karim Mazumder, Mir Hossain Miru, Osman Harun Mahmud Dulal, Jahanara Begum, and others notable attendees also included the former advisor and treasurer of the Diabetic Association of Bangladesh, Major General (Rtd.) Professor Dr. A. R Khan, and Joynal Abedin Hazari, MP.

On June 26, 1994 National Council of the Diabetic Association of Bangladesh granted affiliation to the Diabetic Association of Bangladesh, Feni Branch. Later, District Administration allocated 60 Decimal Government non-agricultural khas land on Mizan Road (present place) to set up the association's office, and about 126 metric tons of wheat was allocated to fill up the low land. According to the plan of the RDC Habibul Kabir Chowdhury, initially, the semi-paved building was constructed, and activities were transferred from the municipality to the association’s own building (present location) on October 1, 1995. On this occasion, a fruitful program was organized at Shaheed Zahir Raihan Auditorium. Mr. A.H.M Nurul Islam, the Deputy Commissioner of Feni presided over the meeting and former advisor and treasurer of the Diabetic Association of Bangladesh, Major General (Rtd.) Professor Dr. A.R Khan was the Chief Guest, and Secretary Gyanendriya Chakma was Special Guest.

On December 06, 1994, former Honorable Prime Minister of the People's Republic of Bangladesh laid the foundation stone of the hospital building to be constructed with the help of the government project. The government project for construction of 25 bedded Diabetes Hospital was considered in the inter-ministerial meeting on 26/06/1995 and a joint meeting on the original PCP was held on 09/08/1995. In the said ECNEC meeting, the project specification for setting up 25-beded diabetes hospitals in Sylhet, Faridpur, Jhenaidah, Bogra, Jessore, Narsingdi and Feni was approved at a cost of 21 crore 69 lacs taka. The construction work of the government project titled Construction of 25-Bed Diabetes Hospital started on May 2, 1997. After the completion of the construction of the three-storied building at a cost of about 2 crore 20 lacs taka, on 1st January 2001, inaugurated newly constructed building. Also inaugurated 25 bedded indoor hospital services on 9th May of the same year.

In 2004, Diabetic Association of Bangladesh provided a model constitution and instructed all branches associations of the country to revise their constitution as independent diabetic association. In accordance with the said model constitution, in the extra general meeting held on 29/10/2004 necessary amendments were made to the constitution of this association. According to the revised constitution, this association was renamed as 'Feni Diabetic Association' instead of Diabetic Association of Bangladesh, Feni Branch.

Feni Diabetic Association/Hospital, the largest voluntary organization of Feni, is located on Mizan Road, the heart of Feni district town. Currently established on 76 Decimal of the land, this association/hospital is spreading light as a place of trust for health services in the region. The hospital has developed very rapidly as it was under the government scheme immediately after its establishment. According to the former secretary general of DAB, Fasih Uddin Mahtab, “Feni Diabetic Association is moving forward at the speed of a rocket”. Due to the competent and courageous role of the former Feni district administration officials and civil surgeons as well as non-government social workers, this hospital prospered in a very short time. Former Deputy Commissioner of Feni Iftekhar Ahmed, A.H.M Nurul Islam, Debashish Nag, Noor Mohammad, A.F.M Solaiman Chowdhury, Masum Khan, and Civil Surgeons Dr. Shamsul Alam, Dr. Md. Mokhlesur Rahman, Dr. Md. Rashiduzzman, Dr. Mainuddin Chowdhury and Dr. Kazi Ismail Amanullah's contribution is undeniable. From the beginning, Diabetic Association of Bangladesh has been giving annual donations. Besides, DAB President National Professor A.K. Azad Khan played a role in financial development by conducting medical camps.

An attempt was made to increase the number of beds by starting the construction of the 4th, 5th, and 6th floors on 1 October 2006. Gradually, 125-bed hospital facility was created by constructing 4, 5 and 6 floors with own financing. On September 6, 2004, the Health Secretary of the Government of the People's Republic of Bangladesh A.F.M Sarwar Kamal inaugurated Blood Bank and expressed his satisfaction.

Although approved number of beds in the hospital is 50. An application has been made to DG Health to increase it to 100 beds. At present, an average of 80/90 patients are admitted daily. Nurul-Rawnak Diabetes Health Service Sub-Centre was started in 2013 at Dhalia of Feni Sadar to deal with the increasing pressure of patients. The current number of registered diabetic patients is 1.5 lac. The present life member of the association is 314. Since its inception 67 patrons/founders/life members have passed away. Currently, 323 officers and employees are engaged in various departments, including 40 doctors and 71 nursing staff.

A pharmacy naming 'Feni Diabetes Hospital Medical Hall' was launched in 2011 to ensure affordable and easy access to medicines for diabetic patients and in-patients of the hospital. Although the service activities were started on a small scale, the pharmacy currently managed by 15 employees has emerged as a large service organization.

In 2013, a Hemodialysis Unit was introduced to alleviate the suffering of dialysis patients in these areas. About 60 lacs Tk. spent to launch this department with complete self-financing. An average of 10 patients are being given dialysis services per day in 5 machines. The recent donation of a machine from Mercantile Bank Ltd. is making it’s possible to provide continuous dialysis services.

In 2017, Honorable Member of The Parliament from Feni-2 Nizam Uddin Hazari donated 20 lacs Tk. to start a CCU in the name of his parents due to an alarming rise in heart disease. A 6-bedded CCU was started there, after naming the ward 'Commissioner Zainal Abdin and Del Afroz Begum'.

When the healthcare system of the whole country was about to collapse during the covid-19 pandemic, Shusen Chandra Shil, general secretary of the association led from the front and set up a separate covid ward with donations from the Haji Nazir Ahmad group. He contacted Alauddin Ahmed Chowdhury Nasim, Chairman of ‘Salahuddin Hosneyara Foundation’, and arranged to start 3 bed ICU in the name of ‘Salahuddin Hosneyara Foundation’. It saved the lives of many critical patients.

Due to the pressure of the patients of Greater Noakhali, Chouddagram of Comilla, Mirsarai of Chittagong, and hilly districts, Feni Diabetes Hospital has suffered from the lack of infrastructure, the executive council of the Feni Diabetic Association has already taken up the project of constructing two 15-storied buildings on the east and western sides of the main building. In between the east building has already been completed with 13-storey roof casting through the project committee. If the west building project will be built with the help of the government project donation the number of beds in the hospital will be increased to 250 and it will be possible to take another step forward in realizing the dream of making a medical college. Diabetic Association of Bangladesh, Feni District Administration, and the Health Department have been cooperating in this work as before.

Feni Diabetic Association is indeed progressing at rocket speed, as the association has been running under dedicated leadership since its inception. Present A.K.M. Shaheed Reza Shimul and Shusen Chandra Shil’s led council are moving ahead with the desired goal of building an excellent medical college with integrity, transparency, and dynamism. We feel proud to be a servant/member of this organization.
